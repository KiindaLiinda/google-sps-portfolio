About Me

I am a 2nd year, Computer Science and Engineering major at the University of California Merced. I am from Los Angeles, California, but my parents are from Mexico. I first learned to code through robotics at the age of 12 and my journey went from there. I then taught myself basic web development, went on to learn C++, Python, and Java, and have built an interest in artificial intelligence and machine learning. On the side, I create gaming content on Twitch and YouTube.

Programs and Projects

Built by Girls WAVE

Built by Girls WAVE is a mentorship program for female and non-binary students to learn about STEM careers. I have been through 4 rounds and learned about various pathways to tech, things my mentors wish they would've known before, and even got a tour of one of my mentor's offices (Netflix HQ in Hollywood, CA)!

She Leads (now Gen She) Entrepreneurship Makeathon

The Entrepreneurship Makeathon is an event for high school female/non binary students to learn about the steps it takes to start a business. We also got to pitch our own ideas to win a mentorship opportunity with a business consulting company. I was one of the 3 grand prize winners for this event.

Google CSSI

Summer of 2019, I spent 3 weeks at the Google LAX campus with 2 of my high school classmates, and a ton of other recent high school graduates. We learned HTML/CSS/JavaScript/Python and Google's App Engine. It was an amazing experience. The link to our final project can be found here.

Hack Merced V

Hack Merced was the second hackathon I've ever attended, and it took place at my university. I spent 36 hours with 3 of my friends, where I put the skills I learned at CSSI to the test and built a web application to help the homeless in Merced. My team and I won the "Best Social Good Hack" category for this hackathon and I will be participating in Hack Merced VI in March with one of my former teammates. our project can be found here and our DevPost can be found here.
